What is Fluoride?

Fluoride is a natural mineral found throughout the earth’s crust and widely distributed in nature. Food products & water also contain it..

How Does Fluoride Work?

Fluoride helps prevent cavities in two different ways:
Fluoride concentrates in the growing bones and developing teeth of children, helping to harden the enamel in baby and adult teeth before they emerge. Fluoride helps to harden the enamel on adult teeth that have already emerged. Fluoride works during the demineralization and remineralization process that naturally occurs in your mouth. Topical fluoride application at age intervals of 3 yrs, 7 yrs, 11 yrs and 13 yrs prevents tooth decay and makes your child’s teeth fluoride protected.
Ask your dentist to give your child the fluoride protection..

What should be the appropriate age for orthodontic treatment?

As such, there is no fixed age for orthodontic treatment. Age of the patient may vary from 6yrs to 45 years depending upon the malocclusion. Some malocclusions are simple which require only dental correction. They can be treated even after cessation of growth. Malocclusions which require bony corrections or growth modifications need initiation of the treatment at an early age. So, parents should be cautions enough to regularly consult an orthodontist for the best results of the child.

I have been advised extraction of some teeth for orthodontic corrections. Why should teeth be extracted?

When there is less space available in the mouth and the total material of the dentition is more, then they express in two ways-

  • Irregular/ Crowding of teeth.
  • Proclination / Teeth coming out.

So, if we need to realign them in proper place where they should be exactly, then we need to create space. This space is created by removing some teeth and the most common being 1 Because of their small size and their position in the mouth (not visible from front on smiling and not much helpful while chewing). So, research says that they are most common tooth to be extracted for orthodontic purposes.

Is the orthodontic treatment painful?

No, the orthodontic treatment is not painful. However, in some patients, the teeth may become slightly sensitive whenever the wire is changed. But if the patient follows all the instructions of his doctor properly, then all these minor problems can be minimized.

What is lingual orthodontics?

Lingual orthodontics is that branch of orthodontics in which braces are placed on the inner aspect of teeth and the whole treatment is carried out from there. The main advantage of it is that the braces are not visible at all. Nobody will know whether you have braces or not. But there are some disadvantages also.

  • Tongue always comes in contact with it which may cause slight irritation or sometimes.
  • In some patients, the speech may also get slightly altered.
  • Treatment time gets increased from 6 months to 1 year depending upon the case. So, depending upon the priorities the patients should select the type of braces.

Why the orthodontic treatments takes so long?

The physiologic movement of any tooth is a slow process. So, we have to keep the force level for the tooth movement within the physiologic (normal) limits. If we increase the force level beyond that limit, the tooth will feel pathologic pressure and will become mobile instead of showing the movement. This is the main reason that the average orthodontic treatment takes about 1 – 1 ½ years. Certain laceration in tongue. difficult cases even take longer than that.

What are the precautions to be taken during the orthodontic treatment?

The main precautions which a child has to follow during fixed orthodontic treatment is to follow the given diet regime which mainly is staying away from very hard food and sticky food items.

  • The patient has to brush regularly at least three times a day.
  • The child should avoid any type of injury or trauma on face.

What are myofunctional appliances?

Myofunctional appliances are those appliances which mainly act on the muscles of face and slowly change their action. As a result, the direction of the bony growth also changes as required. So, they are mainly used in bony deformities when the child is in growing stage.

What are ceramic braces?

Ceramic braces are tooth-coloured braces which when bonded to the teeth merge with them and are nearly invisible from a distance.

Who is an orthodontist?

Orthodontist is a dental surgeon who has super specialized in the branch of orthodontics. He deals with all the problems related to bone and growth deformities of face and head including both upper and lower jaws. He also deals with irregular and malaligned teeth which need correction either with fixed braces or removable plate. He also treats cleft lip and palate cases as one of the most important member of the team...
